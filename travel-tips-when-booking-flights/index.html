Travel Tips When Booking Flights

With the tourism industry currently more competitive than ever amid such economic uncertainty, many airlines are trying to help maximize their profit margins more than ever. Unfortunately, it’s the unsuspecting and ill-prepared consumer that is so often on the wrong end of things such as hidden charges and extra taxations.

It’s especially concerning for newbie travelers and important that they aim to prepare for all outcomes when traveling to places for the first time or using a different airline that they are used to frequenting. Why? Major and budget airlines rules and regulations vary depending on which airline you are traveling with. So, what are the main things that newbie travelers should look out for? Read on….

Beware of budget airlines

Although budget airline prices may look instantly appealing, they can unfortunately include a whole host of extra charges and taxations on top of the original ticket prices. It’s essential that when booking such flights, you research and read the fine print to make sure you aren’t being taxed for things such as infant charges, EU levy or airport tax, to name a few.

Check if you need to print your boarding card out before you leave for the airport

In 2012, CNCB reported that a certain budget airline was charging customers a significant amount if they failed to turn up to the airport with their boarding cards printed out. It’s a ploy that got the airline a significant amount of bad press, and many customers fell foul of this newfangled rule.

Infant charges

If you are traveling with children, some airlines actually enforce infant charges. The majority of reputable airlines don’t, but you’ll find some of the budget carriers will charge for infants under the age of two. So, if you’re trying to shop around for a bargain, look for the airlines that don’t charge.

Do not exceed the baggage limit, or it will cost you dearly

Airlines made billions in 2013 through people exceeding baggage limits, and budget airlines are the worst for it. Case in point: UK-based company Parking4Less reported in their blog post entitled ‘Beware of Cheap Flights’ that “Easyjet announced it was reducing the amount of ‘guaranteed cabin baggage allowance by 37%.” The alarming thing for travelers is that, announcements like these aren’t well publicized so it reaffirms why all flyers should read the small print on their tickets before flying. The last thing you want happening is to face an unnecessary charge for something so trivial as exceeding the airline’s cabin baggage limit.

All of the aforementioned concerning variables that airlines are “cashing in” on are a little worrying for flyers but hopefully these tips will help our readers from incurring similar unwanted excesses. Just remember: as long as you prepare and do your research, you should be fine and will know just what to prepare or avoid when booking your flight tickets.
