Must visit attractions in Porto

Porto might be Portugal’s second largest city after Lisbon, but it is by no means playing second fiddle to the capital. This once-mercantile port both sprawls outward and stacks upward in colourful, shambling, red-roofed clusters at the banks of the Douro River.

Take time to explore the narrow streets of the old town of Ribeira, charm can be sought amid the dilapidation. If you’re considering an exciting getaway to sun drenched Portugal, look no further than First Choice who offer competitive prices on flights and hotel options to the region.

Livraria Lello
Not one to be flicked past in the guidebook, Livrario Lello is one of the oldest bookstores in Portugal, infamous for its interior; a twisting, opulent heart-stopper for the bibliophile. Its spiralling Art Nouveau staircase is said to have inspired some of the aesthetics behind Harry Potter, but while you might yearn to wield your camera in awe, snapshots are strictly off limits. Settle in for a coffee, some cake and a book in what is commonly considered one of the world’s most beautiful bookshops.

Palacio da Bolsa
The city’s stock exchange building, in the heart of the city’s historic centre may have a slightly surly exterior, but inside, its chambers will melt your heart. Check out the opulent, glinting golden Arab room, or the echoing, skylit space of Pátio das Nações. While it might not be used as a palace, this unmissable venue is certainly fit for a king.

Ponte D Luis
This jaw-dropping yawn of 19th century ingenuity stretches its two levels across the Douro thanks to master engineer Gustave Eiffel. For the best views, wander the length of its top deck at sunset, or take the cable car down to the wine houses of Gaia, where a draught of port will steady any shaken nerves!

Taylor’s Port
It might take a bit of a hike to get there, but taking a guided tour of a port house is a must-do experience when in Porto. Learn about the manufacture of this famous fortified wine, and sample a few glasses of heady tipple, all for less than five Euros. Alternatively, stay for dinner at their rustic restaurant, if you prefer a bottle of red to sip on.
